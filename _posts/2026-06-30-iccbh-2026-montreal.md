The Pediatric MSK Imaging Lab travelled to Montréal for the International Conference on Children’s Bone Health, sharing new work on pediatric fracture healing and bone marrow adiposity during growth.
<!-- excerpt end -->

The Pediatric Musculoskeletal Imaging Lab was excited to attend the 2026 International Conference on Children’s Bone Health (ICCBH) in Montréal. It was a wonderful opportunity to connect with the pediatric bone research community, share new lab work, and come home with fresh ideas for studying the growing skeleton.

Camryn McMillan presented a poster on pediatric fracture healing, highlighting ongoing work to better understand how bone repairs itself during growth. Alana Stahl presented a late-breaking podium presentation on sex-specific differences in bone marrow adiposity during growth, bringing our preclinical bone and marrow research to the ICCBH stage.

And because Montréal knows how to put on a show, we also made time for Cirque du Soleil — a pretty excellent reminder that biomechanics can be both scientifically fascinating and wildly entertaining.
